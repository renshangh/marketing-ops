# Inbound Lead Capture Graph

## Concept
A single Randy OS graph that ingests every inbound channel (Discord, Telegram, WhatsApp, email, LinkedIn/X DMs, site forms) and routes each signal through a repeatable ranking + qualification loop before handing it to an operator. The story shows the broken "scattershot" experience, then flips to the unified graph that keeps every lead within one system while preserving context and accountability.

## Graph copy (main + alternates)
- **Main headline:** "Inbound DMs were a minefield. Randy OS turned them into one graph." 
- **Alternate tighter version:** "No more chasing missed DMs—one graph captures every channel." 
- **Operator version:** "Operators won’t miss a lead when Randy OS routes Slack, email, and Discord to a single queue." 

## Graph nodes + flow (for visual/draft support)
1. **Signal Layer:** Channel icons + labels (Discord, Telegram, WhatsApp, Email/Forms, LinkedIn/X, Web chat). 
2. **Normalization:** Filter + assign urgency (High/Medium), dedupe duplicates, fold in context (previous conversations). 
3. **Agent Graph:** Randy OS matches the lead with a known persona, adds custom fields (company size, need), and highlights the next action. 
4. **Operator Queue:** Single dashboard with one tap accept, follow, or delegate; includes playback of the inbound channel to keep context.
5. **Proof chip:** “+92% inbound coverage, 0 threads lost in the last 2 weeks” or similar.

## Why inbound unification beats "another AI chat"
- "Other AI chats" expect you to reply inside each app; Randy OS turns every channel into structured data and routes it through a repeatable workflow so operators never open multiple tabs again. 
- The graph tracks ownership, time to respond, and escalation, so you can prove reliability to Ops leadership. 
- Operators get actionable context instantly (past replies, disposition tags) rather than a "keep typing" chat window.
