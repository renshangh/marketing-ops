# Narrow Wedge Definition: Recruiting Coordination

## What This Should Not Be
This should **not** be:
- a generic job board
- a broad AI recruiting platform
- a catch-all marketplace for every hiring problem
- a services-heavy recruiting agency disguised as software

If it becomes one of those, it loses focus and becomes easier to copy.

---

## The Stronger Wedge
The best framing is:

**Recruiting coordination infrastructure that starts with structured hiring intelligence and expands into company-side posting, updates, and workflow coordination.**

That means the wedge is not just listings.
The wedge is the gap between:
- public hiring information
- employer job updates
- workflow coordination around active hiring

---

## First Useful Product
A strong first product could be:

### Hiring intelligence + freshness layer
- aggregated public jobs
- normalized and deduplicated listings
- change tracking
- company/job enrichment
- freshness visibility
- structured views by company/role/location

Why this works:
- useful before employer onboarding
- one person can start it
- creates proprietary workflow insight over time
- sets up a transition into direct employer workflows

---

## The Real Product You Are Growing Toward
The real destination is not search.
It is **coordination**.

That could include:
- direct employer posting
- structured job intake
- sync and updates
- stale posting detection
- hiring status nudges
- role maintenance workflows
- recruiter/employer coordination support

So the story becomes:

### Step 1
Organize the market.

### Step 2
Become the easiest place for companies to keep hiring information fresh and structured.

### Step 3
Own one repetitive coordination workflow around active hiring.

---

## The Best Narrow Starting Point
If I were forcing this into one concise wedge, I would define it as:

**A system that helps companies keep hiring information accurate, fresh, structured, and operationally manageable.**

This is better than “job discovery” because it naturally leads into workflow ownership.

---

## Why This Wedge Is Good

### 1. It can start without enterprise permission
You can begin with public data and user value.

### 2. It creates a path to direct company participation
Once the data is useful enough, companies have a reason to push information directly.

### 3. It makes agentic workflows actually useful
Agents are strong at:
- data cleanup
- update detection
- structured intake
- nudges and reminders
- workflow assistance

That is a real coordination layer, not AI decoration.

### 4. It avoids the commodity trap
You do not want to be “another place with job listings.”
You want to own the freshness, structure, and operational coordination around hiring information.

---

## Design Rules

### Rule 1
The data layer is the wedge, not the business.

### Rule 2
Pick one narrow coordination workflow after the data wedge works.

### Rule 3
Do not broaden into all of recruiting too early.

### Rule 4
Sell operational value:
- cleaner job info
- faster updates
- lower admin friction
- better visibility

### Rule 5
Use humans only as fallback, not as the core operating model.

---

## Positioning Line
A clean positioning line could be:

**A recruiting coordination system that keeps hiring information fresh, structured, and easy to manage — first across the market, then inside company workflows.**

---

## Final Take
The narrow wedge is not “recruiting AI.”
The narrow wedge is:

**structured hiring intelligence that evolves into hiring coordination infrastructure.**

That is focused enough to start, broad enough to expand, and specific enough to avoid becoming a weak generic job product.
