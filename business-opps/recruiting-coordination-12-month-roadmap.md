# 12-Month Startup Roadmap: Recruiting Coordination

## Core Thesis
The opportunity is not to build “another job board.”
The opportunity is to build **recruiting coordination infrastructure** that begins with structured hiring intelligence and expands into company-side workflow ownership.

---

## Month 0-2: Define the Wedge

### Goal
Choose a narrow first use case and validate that it creates real value.

### Focus
Start with one tight problem, such as:
- structured hiring intelligence for a niche market
- job freshness and change tracking
- role/company signal enrichment
- recruiting coordination around posting and updates

### Deliverables
- clear ICP definition
- first market segment
- first workflow hypothesis
- list of public data sources
- first quality criteria for useful data

### Main question to answer
What exact pain point makes someone care before the product becomes a full workflow system?

---

## Month 2-4: Build the Data Layer

### Goal
Create a useful hiring intelligence product from crawled and normalized data.

### Product capabilities
- crawl public job sources
- normalize job/company/location/title data
- deduplicate listings
- track freshness and changes
- enrich company/job metadata
- surface useful search and filtering

### Why this matters
This creates value before you need deep enterprise integration.
It also gives you the market map needed to know where to go next.

### Success signal
Users say the product is materially better than generic job search or raw scraping.

---

## Month 4-6: Build Distribution and Feedback Loops

### Goal
Get real users and real usage patterns.

### Focus
- talk to hiring managers, recruiters, operators, and small companies
- learn which data is actually useful
- identify repeated coordination pain
- test whether companies want direct posting or sync options

### Product additions
- alerts
- saved views
- company/job monitoring
- basic workflow notes
- invite flow for employer posting

### Success signal
You begin hearing: “Can I just update/post/manage this directly in your system?”

---

## Month 6-8: Add Company-Side Posting and Sync

### Goal
Turn the product from pure intelligence into a workflow surface.

### Product capabilities
- direct employer posting
- structured job intake
- sync/update workflows
- editing and freshness control
- agent-assisted posting cleanup
- status updates for active roles

### Why this matters
This is the transition point from data product to coordination product.

### Success signal
Companies use the system not just to observe the market, but to manage live hiring information.

---

## Month 8-10: Expand into Coordination Workflows

### Goal
Own one painful workflow after posting.

### Potential workflow wedges
- intake clarification
- hiring status updates
- recruiter/employer communication
- interview coordination support
- candidate pipeline hygiene
- stale-role cleanup and reminders

### Product principle
Choose one workflow with:
- high frequency
- painful repetition
- measurable value
- enough standardization to avoid custom services

### Success signal
You are no longer just a data layer. You are improving operations.

---

## Month 10-12: Tighten the Product and Prove ROI

### Goal
Turn a promising workflow tool into a credible business.

### Focus
- measure time saved
- measure improved freshness/accuracy
- measure reduced coordination friction
- measure posting/update responsiveness
- identify the strongest use case to double down on

### Product additions
- dashboards
- usage reporting
- workflow visibility
- human fallback / review queues
- permissions and role controls where needed

### Success signal
Customers can clearly describe the ROI in operational or business terms.

---

## Strategic Rules for the Whole Year

### Rule 1: Do not stop at aggregation
The data layer is a wedge, not the destination.

### Rule 2: Own one workflow before expanding
Do not try to automate all of recruiting at once.

### Rule 3: Productize aggressively
Avoid turning into a custom setup shop.

### Rule 4: Use agents where they reduce coordination burden
Good uses include:
- posting cleanup
- update reminders
- change detection
- structured intake help
- workflow nudges
- communication assistance

### Rule 5: Measure boring operational value
The company wins on:
- reduced staleness
- faster updates
- less admin work
- better visibility
- cleaner coordination

Not on hype.

---

## End-of-Year Outcome to Aim For
By month 12, the ideal position is:
- you own a useful hiring intelligence layer
- some companies directly post or sync through you
- you have one strong workflow wedge in recruiting coordination
- customers can explain the ROI clearly
- the product is evolving from data infrastructure into workflow infrastructure

That is a much stronger company than “AI job board.”
