# X Graphic System

A fast, repeatable visual system for X posts about OpenClaw, AI agents, automation, GTM, and marketing ops.

## Goal

Turn plain-text posts into more scroll-stopping posts using lightweight visuals that are:
- fast to produce
- easy to repeat
- brand-safe
- useful, not decorative

## Core Principle

For X, the visual should do one of four jobs:
1. prove something
2. explain something
3. simplify something
4. make the hook easier to understand at a glance

If the image does none of those, skip it.

---

## The 4 Best Graphic Formats

### 1. Quote Card
Best for:
- strong contrarian takes
- concise lessons
- founder/operator opinions

Structure:
- large headline quote
- short supporting line
- small brand mark / handle
- clean background

Example headline:
- Speed + consistency > one perfect workflow

Example support line:
- The teams that win with AI ship, measure, and iterate faster than everyone else.

Use when:
- the post is idea-led
- no hard proof screenshot is available

---

### 2. Workflow Card
Best for:
- OpenClaw workflows
- GTM systems
- agent orchestration
- process explainers

Structure:
- title at top
- 3 to 5 steps in a horizontal or vertical flow
- arrows or numbered blocks
- one-line outcome at bottom

Example:
- Scan top 10 posts
- Reply to relevant threads
- Publish 1 original post
- Track engagement patterns

Footer:
- Speed compounds when the loop is daily.

Use when:
- teaching a repeatable process
- showing how your operation works

---

### 3. Proof / Metrics Card
Best for:
- results
- tests
- experiments
- conversion or engagement updates

Structure:
- one big metric or result
- 2 to 4 smaller supporting numbers
- short label for context
- optional date range

Example:
- 10 replies sent
- 1 original post published
- 1 workflow improved
- 1 lesson captured

Use when:
- you have concrete numbers
- you want credibility quickly

---

### 4. Before / After Card
Best for:
- manual vs agent-assisted workflows
- old process vs new process
- time savings or quality improvements

Structure:
- split layout
- left = before
- right = after
- one line summarizing the delta

Example:
Before:
- manual post research
- inconsistent replies
- no tracking loop

After:
- daily scan system
- structured engagement
- repeatable publishing rhythm

Use when:
- you want to show transformation
- you want high clarity fast

## Recommended Posting Stack

### Tier 1: Fastest daily system
Use these most often:
- workflow card
- quote card

Why:
- easiest to create
- matches OpenClaw / AI ops content well
- doesn’t require heavy design or screenshots

### Tier 2: Credibility system
Use when possible:
- proof / metrics card
- annotated screenshot

Why:
- proof beats claims
- stronger trust and engagement

### Tier 3: Higher effort, bigger upside
Use selectively:
- mini carousel
- polished infographic
- branded diagram

Why:
- better for bigger ideas
- more effort, so use for stronger posts

## Visual Style Direction

### Tone
- clean
- modern
- technical but not cluttered
- operator energy, not hype-beast energy

### Design language
- dark background or soft neutral background
- 1 strong accent color
- bold headline
- minimal extra decoration
- boxes, lines, and spacing instead of visual noise

### Good visual cues for this brand
- flow arrows
- simple diagrams
- cards/panels
- terminal / dashboard / system-inspired layout
- subtle grid texture

Avoid:
- stock-photo look
- over-designed gradients everywhere
- too much tiny text
- generic AI art that adds no meaning

---

## X-Specific Rules

- prioritize readability on mobile first
- one visual = one idea
- keep headline large enough to read in feed preview
- avoid dense paragraphs inside the graphic
- if text is long, make it a carousel instead
- screenshots should be cropped tightly and annotated if needed

Safe starting size:
- 1600x900 or 1200x675 for landscape cards
- 1080x1080 for square quote/proof cards

Best default for your niche:
- 1600x900 workflow or proof card
