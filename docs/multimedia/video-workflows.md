# Video Workflow Template

## Purpose
Create a repeatable short-form video workflow for OpenClaw social content.

## Goal
Turn a post idea into a video plan that is ready for scripting, editing, and review.

## Inputs
- topic or angle
- audience
- desired platform
- core hook
- proof point or story point
- caption style
- source footage or concept notes

## Workflow
1. define the hook
2. identify the conflict or tension
3. choose the format
4. map the first 5 to 15 seconds
5. draft the beat structure
6. define on-screen captions
7. keep the final clip within the standard duration
8. review the frame pacing
9. produce or edit the clip
10. extract review frames if needed
11. finalize the export

## Hook rules
- start with the problem, not the intro
- use a conflict-first opening
- remove dead air
- keep the first line immediate and specific

## Beat map
Use a simple beat structure:
- beat 1, hook
- beat 2, context
- beat 3, core point
- beat 4, example or proof
- beat 5, close or CTA

## Caption rules
- keep captions large
- keep them high-contrast
- keep them centered and mobile readable
- avoid dense paragraphs

## Review rules
- inspect the first 5 to 15 seconds first
- check for pacing drift
- check for readability
- check for unnecessary silence
- check for weak visual framing
- confirm the clip lands in the 10 to 15 second standard window, with 12 seconds as the default

## Output
The final output should include:
- video concept
- hook line
- beat map
- caption guidance
- review notes
- export or post notes

## Audio add-on workflow
- finalize the script first
- generate MP3 narration with the approved TTS tool
- merge narration with the clip using ffmpeg
- review sync and timing before publish


## Notes
If the video needs deeper production, split the work into:
- concept draft
- script draft
- frame review
- edit revision

## Standard clip rule
- default clip duration is 12 seconds
- acceptable clip duration range is 10 to 15 seconds
- keep motion subtle and readable
